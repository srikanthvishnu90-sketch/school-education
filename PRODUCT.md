# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students use plumb after a lesson to reflect on what they understood, how the work felt, and what they will do next. Teachers use it to turn lesson context and student reflections into an actionable class brief, then compare those reflections with later evidence. Counselors receive only deterministic safety escalations when a student may need immediate human support.

## Product Purpose

plumb is a classroom reflection and learning-intelligence tool that helps students build accurate academic self-knowledge without ranking, diagnosis, or surveillance. It connects how a lesson felt with how it was understood, gives each student one controllable next step, and gives the teacher observed, task-focused evidence they can act on. Success means better student ownership and more useful teacher follow-up, not more model judgment.

## Brand Personality

Calm, trustworthy, and student-owned. The product should feel private enough for honest reflection, precise enough for educators to trust, and supportive without becoming sentimental, clinical, or reward-driven.

## Anti-references

- Diagnostic, ranking, or administrative-surveillance interfaces that turn reflection into a verdict about the student.
- Gamified learning products that use points, streaks, confetti, or correctness rewards.
- Red/green performance semantics or emotionally prescriptive feedback.
- Loud AI-chat styling, ornamental gradients, glass effects, or novelty interactions that compete with the conversation.
- Dense institutional forms that make a student feel processed rather than heard.

## Design Principles

- Keep the student in control: explain what is happening, provide a way out, and end with a next step they choose.
- Make evidence actionable: every insight should connect to the work, a specific cause, or a concrete follow-up.
- Treat reflection as practice, not measurement: self-report helps a student notice and explain an episode, but evidence and outcomes remain the source of truth for decisions.
- Ask about concrete recent moments, never stable traits; use neutral wording and make “I’m not sure” or an optional skip a valid response.
- Practice calm precision: one decision at a time, direct language, restrained emphasis, and familiar controls.
- Treat AI as labor, never judgment: models may draft and classify, while deterministic rules and people own outcomes and safety.
- Preserve dignity under stress: safety escalation should be supportive, private, and immediately understandable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Core flows should meet WCAG 2.2 AA, remain keyboard operable, preserve visible focus, and maintain readable text and control contrast. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Never rely on red/green or color alone to communicate accuracy, emotion, safety, or completion. Copy must stay task-focused and understandable to adolescents without assuming high emotional or academic vocabulary.
